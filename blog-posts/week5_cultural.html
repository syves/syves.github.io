Pairing is Caring

Do I like pairing?

  • Yes. I didn't think I would.

  • I like sharing ideas.

  • I learn a great deal by seeing other peoples approaches to solving problems

  • I sometimes get unstuck with others help faster than I would on my own...yeah most of the time!

  • I enjoy putting people at ease.

  • I get to practice listening. Sometimes I get to excited and interupt them, or finish their sentences. I also am focusing on listening to every word they say and saying it back in my head, to stop my mind from formulating a response.I'm slowing it down.

  • Sometimes it is difficult to pair remotely, with technical problems interfering with actually writing code.

  • It is more difficult to gage how someone is doing, following along, or feeling long distance.

  • I've learned that most people appreciate my communication style.

  • I am heard and I must remember to let the other person have a say. I've been more proactive by asking the other person what approach they would like to take, drive or navigate and checking in with how they are feeling.

      • Feedback

    • When I read positive feedback I feel grateful for this opportunity to participate, learn and facilitate others learning.

    • I feel good about my communication skills.

    • I did get one piece of feedback that surprised me. I was unaware that I was not giving my pair enough say in matters. I feel bad that I was not thoughtful. I have been careful to give my pairs more time to discuss their approach and have had good feed back since then.Sorry pair!!

    • It is sometimes difficult to come up with constructive feed back after a positive pair, in which the other person both communicates well and is more technically skilled than I am.

    • It was most difficult to write feedback when I felt my pair would be embarrassed by what I wrote. But kindness is honesty, and our honest feed back helps us to see what we can improve, which may actually effect our ability to be hired.
